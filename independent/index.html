Case study — live product

weduediligence.com

Know every lien before you bid. Foreclosure intelligence that removes the guesswork buyers face when auction timelines don't allow real diligence.

weduediligence.com

The live site — click through to visit.

Problem

Foreclosure auctions compress a decision that normally takes weeks of diligence into days — sometimes minutes. Buyers had no fast way to vet a property before bidding: title work and professional diligence were too slow and too expensive for auction timelines, so bidders leaned on incomplete public records and priced the uncertainty into every bid, or didn't price it at all. The result is a market where the best-informed bidder wins and everyone else is gambling on liens they can't see.

What was built

weduediligence.com is a paywalled, Zillow-like listing platform for foreclosure properties, currently covering Dallas, Denton, and Collin counties in Texas. Every listing layers three sources of truth: automated data pulls from public records, manual entry and human review, and an AI-generated assessment — title status, liens, repair estimates, and a risk score — so a buyer understands a property's true position before ever stepping into the auction room. The platform has analyzed 200+ properties, adds new listings weekly, and sells access from $149/month.

My specific role

Sole developer and operator. I built the product end to end and own everything around it — the research process behind each listing, the pricing, and the client relationship.

Outcome

The product is live with a paying client. The roadmap on the site lists California and New York as the next markets.
