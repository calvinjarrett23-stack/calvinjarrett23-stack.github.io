Case study

NEXUS

Find the right internal expert in seconds, instead of guessing who to ask.

Problem

Large consulting organizations run on institutional knowledge — but knowledge is only useful if you can find the person who holds it. In practice, IBM employees, and interns especially, had no reliable way to identify the right internal expert for a given question. The expertise existed somewhere in a workforce of hundreds of thousands; locating it meant asking around, guessing from org charts, and losing hours or days to a search problem that software should be solving.

What was built

NEXUS treats that search problem as a routing problem. It is a full-stack retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) platform — a React front end over a Node backend and PostgreSQL with the pgvector extension for embedding search, integrated with IBM ICA. Ask it a question and it does more than return documents: it routes you to the internal experts most likely to answer, each with a confidence score, and renders the organization's expertise as an interactive knowledge-graph visualization you can explore. A PowerPoint generation pipeline turns findings into client-ready output directly from the platform.

My specific role

I led the team and did all of the development myself — the RAG pipeline, the expert-routing and confidence-scoring logic, the knowledge-graph visualization, and the PowerPoint generation pipeline.

Outcome

NEXUS was presented at IBM's intern competition; no formal placement or result has been announced yet.
