Case study

Predictive Cash Flow

Replaced a manual, unreliable cash-forecasting process with an automated, rules-driven model in Oracle EPM.

Problem

Cash is the one forecast a finance team cannot afford to get wrong — and the client was producing theirs by hand. The process was manual and spreadsheet-based: slow to assemble, dependent on whoever maintained the workbook, and unreliable as a basis for forward-looking decisions. The client needed daily forward visibility into cash, produced by a system rather than a spreadsheet.

What was built

A predictive daily cash flow solution inside Oracle EPM, built in Calculation Manager on the OEP_DCSH BSO cube. The model is designed to encode how the business actually behaves rather than extrapolate a trend line:

  • Day-of-week conditional rules capture the weekly rhythm of cash inflows and outflows, so a Tuesday forecasts like a Tuesday.
  • SOFR loan-schedule tracking follows floating-rate debt service, with interest-rate autofill so the schedule stays current without manual re-entry.
  • Beginning-cash-balance seeding anchors each day's forecast to actual positions, so errors don't compound down the calendar.

My specific role

Built jointly with one other intern — a two-person build from rule design through delivery.

Outcome

The solution was built and delivered to the client; adoption into the finance team's day-to-day routine is still in progress.
